Aadhaar is a 12-digit unique identity number that can be obtained voluntarily by residents of India, based on their biometric and demographic data. 
The data is collected by the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI), a statutory authority established in January 2009 by the Government of India.

How to apply for Aadhaar:
1. Find an Aadhaar Enrollment Center near you.
2. Fill the enrollment form.
3. Provide biometrics (fingerprints and iris scan).
4. Provide Proof of Identity (POI) and Proof of Address (POA).
5. You will receive an acknowledgment slip with an Enrolment ID.

Documents needed:
- Proof of Identity: Voter ID, Passport, PAN Card, Ration Card.
- Proof of Address: Bank Statement, Electricity Bill, Water Bill.

Official Portal: https://uidai.gov.in
Processing Time: 15 to 30 days.
Fees: Free for first-time enrollment. Mandatory biometric update is also free.
Update Aadhaar Address Online: You can update your address online through the 'myAadhaar' portal by providing valid proof of address.
